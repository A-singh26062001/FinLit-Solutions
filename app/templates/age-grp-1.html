What is financial literacy, and why is it important for me as an under 18?
Financial literacy means having the knowledge and skills to make informed decisions about money, budgeting, saving, and investing. It's important for young people like you because it helps you build a strong foundation for your financial future, make wise financial choices, and achieve your goals.
Can I invest my money even if I'm under 18?
Yes, you can! While you may not be able to open traditional investment accounts, you can learn about investment concepts and practice investing through simulated platforms. Our investment section can guide you through the basics.
What is a Student Loan?
A student loan is money borrowed to pay for educational expenses, such as tuition, books, and living costs. It's repaid with interest after the borrower completes their education.
What is Credit Score?
A credit score is a numerical representation of your creditworthiness. Lenders use it to assess your ability to repay loans. Higher scores indicate better credit management and can lead to lower interest rates.
What is a Bond?
A bond is a debt security issued by governments or corporations. When you buy a bond, you're essentially lending money in exchange for periodic interest payments and the return of the principal when the bond matures.
