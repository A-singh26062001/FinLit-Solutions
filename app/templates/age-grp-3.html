What is a pension scheme, and why is it important for individuals planning for retirement?
A pension scheme is a financial arrangement designed to provide income during retirement. It's important because it ensures individuals have a steady source of income after they stop working, helping them maintain their quality of life and cover expenses in their golden years.
What is the difference between a Defined Benefit (DB) and a Defined Contribution (DC) pension scheme?
In a Defined Benefit scheme, the employer promises a fixed pension amount based on factors like years of service and salary. In a Defined Contribution scheme, the individual contributes a set amount, and the eventual pension depends on how well the investments perform.
What steps can individuals take to ensure their pension fund is on track for a comfortable retirement?
Individuals should regularly review their pension fund's performance, adjust contributions as their financial situation changes, and consider consulting a financial advisor to ensure they're on track to meet their retirement goals.
What is Credit Score?
A credit score is a numerical representation of your creditworthiness. Lenders use it to assess your ability to repay loans. Higher scores indicate better credit management and can lead to lower interest rates.
What is a Bond?
A bond is a debt security issued by governments or corporations. When you buy a bond, you're essentially lending money in exchange for periodic interest payments and the return of the principal when the bond matures.

Benefits of NPS
  • Long-Term Savings for Retirement: NPS is designed to provide a regular income during retirement. It encourages individuals to save consistently over their working years, ensuring a financially secure retirement.
  • Tax Benefits:NPS offers tax benefits under different sections of the Income Tax Act. Contributions to NPS up to ₹1.5 lakh are eligible for deduction under Section 80C, and an additional deduction of up to ₹50,000 is available under Section 80CCD(1B) for contributions to the NPS Tier 1 account.
